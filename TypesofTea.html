Black Teas are bold and down to earth

Black tea is loved by cultures around the world for its bold, earthy taste and its energizing and hydrating effects. Black tea is permitted to oxidize or ferment slightly after processing, unlike green and white teas, bringing out the richer flavor profile of the tea.

  • To help you get moving, calorie-free black tea is filled with lots of caffeine; one serving contains between 50 to 70 mg of caffeine.
  • Enjoyed all day long, both hot and iced, black tea is a rare hydration solution that promises to quench the thirst while re-energizing both body and mind.

White Teas are Light and Soft

Handpicked and handmade tea leaves that represent the delicacy of softness. And it's like that in tea. True White Tea is handpicked before the sun rises, made from the tender bud of a special variety of the tea plant, Camellia Sinensis. This tea is organically grown, as it is hand picked and plucked and then leaves are dried / withered in the sunlight.

  • Handpicked and handmade tea leaves that represent the delicacy of softness
  • This is the tea Goldilocks - a minor aberration makes a clearly lower quality tea - all situations must be 'about right.'
